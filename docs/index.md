# ViGOR Bioinformatics Course

---
## Welcome

Welcome to the **ViGOR Bioinformatics Short Course**, a five-day intensive programme designed to equip researchers, laboratory scientists, epidemiologists, and public health professionals with practical bioinformatics skills for infectious disease surveillance and outbreak response.

The course combines interactive lectures with hands-on practical sessions, guiding participants from the fundamentals of bioinformatics to genome assembly, metagenomics, phylogenetics, and workflow development for pathogen genomics.

---
## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course the course participants will be able to:

- Explain the role of bioinformatics in outbreak response.
- Navigate Linux command-line environments.
- Perform pathogen sequence quality control.
- Conduct genome assembly and phylogenetics analysis.
- Analyse metagenomic datasets.
- Build reproducible bioinformatics workflows.
- Interpret genomic data for public health decision-making.

---

## Target Audiences

This course is aimed at Researchers, Laboratory Scientists, Bioinformaticians and Public Health Professionals who are at the frontline of Outbreak response and Genomic surveillance.

---

## Prerequisites

While foundational concepts are introduced throughout the course, participants are expected to have:

 * A basic understanding of molecular biology and genetics
 * A basic experience using linux commandline
 * A laptop meeting the recommended software requirements

Participants who are new to linux commandline are encouraged to complete:

*The Carpentries* :[Introduction to commandline for Genomics](https://datacarpentry.github.io/shell-genomics)
